Beta - TrailBlazer Project Tutorial

Your task is to identify cancer cells.

These are uneven, often larger cells with a mottled or blotchy surface like the ones highlighted below.

Here's another example of cancer cells

You will see other cell types which you should ignore.

These include the smaller, denser cells and the stretched wispy cells, some of these are highlighted below. We’re not looking for these cells.

Now it’s your turn.

Take a look at the image below. We’d like you to identify which areas of the grid contain cancer cells. Analyse each grid segment and decide if it contains cancer.

Click once for cancer cells

Once you’ve decided you can mark the section as follows: Yes, there are cancer cells – click once to turn the segment green

Click twice for non-cancer cells

No, there are no cancer cells – click twice to turn the segment red

Click again for blank segments


There are no cells in this segment – click again to turn the segment blank.
Don’t worry if a particular segment is very difficult or you aren’t sure. Sometimes the specialists can find this analysis tricky – so just give it your best shot!
