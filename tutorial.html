Your task is to identify cancer cells.

You will see other cell types which you should ignore.

These include the smaller, denser cells and the stretched wispy cells, some of these are highlighted below. We’re not looking for these cells.

Here's an example of what you have to do

Take a look at the image on the left. We’d like you to identify which grid squares contain cancer cells. Analyse each grid square and decide if it contains cancer.

Squares containing cancer cells should be highlighted red

Once you’ve decided you can mark the square as follows: Yes, there are cancer cells – click once to turn the square red

Squares not containing cancer cells should be highlighted green

Once you’ve decided you can mark the square as follows: No, there are not cancer cells – click twice to turn the square green
